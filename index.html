Welcome to Svaneti

Breathtakingly wild and mysterious, Svaneti is an ancient land locked in the Caucasus, so remote that it was never tamed by any ruler. Uniquely picturesque villages and snow-covered, 4000m-plus peaks rising above flower-strewn alpine meadows provide a superb backdrop to the many walking trails. Around 175 towers, most originally built between the 9th and 13th centuries, survive here today.

Svan towers

Svan towers refers to the tower houses built as defensive dwellings, common in the Georgian historical region of Svaneti.

USHBA

Ushba is one of the most notable peaks of the Caucasus Mountains. It is located in the Svaneti region of Georgia, just south of the border with the Kabardino-Balkaria region of Russia.

WHERE IS SVANETI, ANYWAY?

Not familiar with Georgia's Geography? Svaneti is in the northwestern part of the country, along the southern slopes of Greater Caucasus mountains. Racha is bordered to the east, Lechkhumi and Samegrelo to the south, Abkhazia to the west, Karachay and Balkaria to the north.
